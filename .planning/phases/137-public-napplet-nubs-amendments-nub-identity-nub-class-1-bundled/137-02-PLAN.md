    The `report-to` directive and the shell's MUST-process-reports obligation do NOT together constitute a blocking mechanism. CSP violation reports are delivered AFTER the browser has already blocked the offending load or inline-script execution — the report is a notification of what was prevented, not a consent-gate. Violation reports are the mechanism by which a shell LEARNS that a napplet was the subject of an injection attempt or a policy-violating inline load; they give the shell evidence to act on (per the policy-latitude bullet under Shell Responsibilities), but they do not prevent the attempted violation from being observed by the browser in the first place.

    Browsers do NOT deliver reports for extension-originated injections that target the page's main world (for example, `chrome.scripting.executeScript({world:'MAIN'})`) because such injections bypass page CSP entirely. No `securitypolicyviolation` fires, no report is POSTed, and no napplet-side detection is possible. The structural mitigation — and the reason NUB-CLASS-1 is the load-bearing class for any future plaintext-handling NUB — is `connect-src 'none'`: any plaintext obtained inside a NUB-CLASS-1 frame, by any mechanism, cannot be exfiltrated over the network from within that frame because `connect-src 'none'` blocks fetch, XHR, WebSocket, EventSource, and sendBeacon. This is the honest boundary; this NUB does not claim `report-to` plugs the main-world-extension hole.
